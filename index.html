About Me

Google Scholar

I am a senior research scientist at Samsung Research America, working on efficient model design and training for natural language processing (NLP) and computer vision (CV) models. I received my Machine Learning Ph.D. in the School of Interactive Computing at Georgia Tech. I was advised by Zsolt Kira, who leads the RobotIcs Perception and Learning (RIPL) lab.

My PhD dissertation specifically focused on continual adaptation of strongly pre-trained models with an emphasis on solutions which protect data privacy and reduce compute. Check out my dissertation pdf, my public defense slides, or my dissertation defense video!

Projects

*
All Vision + Language Vision

Continual Diffusion: Continual Customization of Text-to-Image Diffusion with C-LoRA

We learn to generate text-conditioned images of new concepts in a sequential manner (i.e., continual learning) without forgetting.

CODA-Prompt: COntinual Decomposed Attention-based Prompting for Rehearsal-Free Continual Learning

We learn decomposed prompts for rehearsal-free continual learning.

ConStruct-VL: Data-Free Continual Structured VL Concepts Learning

We continuously adapt BLIP to new VL concepts.

Always Be Dreaming: A New Approach for Data-Free Class-Incremental Learning

Data-Free Class-Incremental Learning, where an agent must learn new concepts over time without storing generators or training data from past tasks

Memory-Efficient Semi-Supervised Continual Learning: The World is its Own Replay Buffer

Realistic Semi-Supervised Continual Learning, where data distributions reflect object class correlations between, and among, the labeled and unlabeled data distributions

Unsupervised Progressive Learning and the STAM Architecture

Learning data representations from a non-stationary stream of unlabeled data for downstream classification tasks
